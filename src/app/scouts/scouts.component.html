Are You Scouting the Right Player?

Let’s say you’re scouting for a new winger. You look at the aggregated xT (Expected Threat) values and see that Player A has a high xT. You might think, "Great, this player creates high-value actions!" But in reality, Player A might have been in the right place at the right time due to luck or team dynamics, not because of their individual skill.

By ignoring player identity during training the ML models, you lose the ability to distinguish between skill and context. This can lead to overvaluing players who benefit from team tactics or luck, and undervaluing players who are truly skilled but play in less favorable conditions.

The Challenge of Context in Player Evaluation

This is where permutation invariant sorting schemes and Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) come into play. These approaches ensure that the model understands the context of each action while respecting the fact that player identities matter. For example, a player’s ability to create high xT actions should be evaluated based on their individual skill, not just their position on the field.

If you’re a scout or analyst, your goal is to identify players who can consistently create high-value actions based on their individual skills, not just their team’s dynamics. By addressing permutation invariance, you can accurately assess a player’s ability to create high xT actions, identify undervalued players in less dominant teams, and make smarter transfer decisions by understanding how a player’s skills fit into your team’s tactics.
